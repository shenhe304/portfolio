About The Project

About the Color

Blue / Naivy
Trust, Dependability, Logic
Why I choose the deep blue
Unlike Japan, associating pink with femininity in some overseas cultures can be perceived as discriminatory. Therefore, we chose the main color based on the meanings it carries rather than gender stereotypes.

Details

Main Visual
Placed the words 'Divorce, Children, Money' prominently at the center to draw attention. Positioned an image on the background to convey the idea of divorce through a third party.
Small Message
Since it's a landing page, I wanted visitors to read the page like a story, with their eyes naturally flowing downward. Therefore, I separated 'We are here to support you' from the main visual for emphasis.
Empathy Zone
Lowering the tone to make it clear at a glance that you are dealing with concerns. To make it more relatable, a section of empathy was placed just below the main visual.
Firm Introduction Zone
Placed after the Empathy Zone to clearly explain what the law firm can do and make it easily understandable, especially for those unfamiliar with law firms.
The final push
Placed reviews and a message from the firm as the final push for those who are hesitating to seek advice.

Call to Action Section

To acquire more customers, it is essential to increase inquiries. To make the contact methods clear, the CTA has been highlighted with a different color for better visibility.

The differences compared to Japanese design

In Japan, the legal industry tends to have a higher volume of text, including explanations and annotations, which is aimed at preventing misunderstandings and complaints. In designing, I take into consideration the placement of text, spacing, and readability across various devices to ensure that the text doesn't appear overwhelming.

In English-speaking websites, emphasis is placed on a stylish appearance, and the amount of text is generally less compared to Japan. In actual creation, I find that there is greater freedom in design. Accordingly, I pay close attention to user eye movement, ease of clicking, and other factors to enhance user experience.
