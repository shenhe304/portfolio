About The Color

In Canada, unlike Japan, there are no specific colors associated with genders. Therefore, I chose colors based on their meanings and references from competitors, as there is no conventional association of colors with gender in the Canadian context.

From Google search

These are keywords related to "law firm" that are searched on Google.It is evident that in addition to expertise and achievements, there is an emphasis on personality traits such as communication skills and approachability.

This web page is for advertising purposes, so it will be displayed on the screens of people who are researching divorce or lawyers. Therefore, I chose blue tones that convey a sense of expertise and reliability at a glance. Additionally, blue colors can express feelings of loneliness and solitude, making it a perfect match for individuals struggling with divorce who are looking for a trustworthy lawyer.

Result

By choosing a calm tone of navy within the blue spectrum, it was intended to enhance a sense of calmness and trust.

Measures to prevent user abandonment"

  • To clarify the target audience of this web page, we brought the "concerns" section first to have users perceive it as something personal to them.
  • The purpose is to prevent user dropout.

Making the background of the section where concerns are expressed trapezoidal serves the purpose of an arrow, guiding the eyes downward. Also, as you scroll, the lawyer's photo gradually becomes visible, creating a design that easily conveys the image of "resolving concerns.

About us section

When looking for a lawyer, achievements and personality are also valued. To lower the threshold of lawyers and make it more approachable, we adopted profiles with photos. To ensure the lawyer's face is not blurred, we used icons to display achievements and designed it visually to avoid confusion.

Differences from the Japanese design

In Japan, there is a greater amount of text, including annotations, to prevent complaints and unnecessary troubles. When I looked at the websites of some Canadian law firms, there was less text. This allows customers to find the necessary information quickly.

I believe these differences stem from cultural distinctions between customers and service providers. In Japan, there is a culture of expecting high-quality service, and detailed information and explanations are considered essential to prevent unnecessary complaints.

Considering the amount of text, UI design was also taken into account in Japan.

This experience will be valuable when working in Canada, providing insights into adapting to different cultural expectations, especially in terms of communication and information presentation.
