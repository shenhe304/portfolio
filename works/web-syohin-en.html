About The Project

Details

Main Visual
Using a photo of a lawyer looking directly at the user conveys a sense of direct communication and engagement.
Using icons and images
Using an icon can help soften the formal image of a law firm, while incorporating photos can make the content more relatable and personal for the audience.
Blog Section
Implemented a feature where users can switch between tabs by clicking on each respective service.
Making information easily accessible for users seeking specific content.
Reducing vertical length to prevent mobile users from disengaging.
News section
The benefit of incorporating a news section is not only to inform users about important events at the law firm but also to demonstrate that the legal practice is actively and efficiently operating.
Contact form
designed the layout to include an embedded email form to lower the barrier for inquiries to the law firm, making it more convenient for users to get in touch.

The differences compared to Japanese design

In the project in Japan, the design was constrained due to the unique requirement of handing over the website to another department. Despite these limitations, I collaborated with the director and met the requirements of other departments to create a user-friendly site.

For the English site, I opted for a completely different design from the Japanese version. The site uses only two colors, with the intention of emphasizing specific areas in red, making it clear that the services go beyond debt consolidation. This design choice allowed for a distinct visual identity.
