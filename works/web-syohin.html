The official website design for the launch of a new service in my previous company. In order to address instances of defamation on social media and to initiate a legal service for handling issues such as unpaid rent, a new official website is required.

GOAL

I have taken on the responsibility of designing a new website as a new service is being launched, marking a departure from the unchanged lineup of core services over the past few years. Given that the department responsible for web updates lacks expertise in web design, I opted for creating it on WordPress. It was essential to design each block with simplicity and versatility in mind.

The goal was to convey the range of services offered in a clear and straightforward manner to the users. Additionally, for the web updating team, particularly those less familiar with web design, I aimed to create a design that clearly indicates which design to use for each block in a simple and understandable way.

To guide users on where to click and what to focus on, I maintained a unified color scheme to ensure that users' attention remains focused without distractions.

Details

User-Friendly design:
A user-friendly interface that is understandable for both users and administrators.
Prompting action:
Increasing user inquiries by making slight design modifications to the call-to-action (CTA).
Responsive Design:
Considering the Design for Viewing on Smartphones and iPads.

TEAM WORK

We formed a four-person team comprising a web director, web writer, engineer, and myself to create our website.
The structure was overseen by the director, with the writer handling the text, the engineer handling the coding, and myself in charge of the design.

Given the extensive verification processes inherent in the legal industry, we managed to complete the website launch within a tight 2-month timeframe.
